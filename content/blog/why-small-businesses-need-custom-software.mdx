You've been there. Your team signs up for the latest productivity tool, project management platform, or CRM. Everyone's excited. The demo looked perfect.

Two months later? You're fighting the software instead of using it. Features you need are buried in enterprise plans. Your workflow doesn't match their assumptions. Your team invents workarounds.

## The SaaS Trap

Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) tools are built for the **average** customer. That means they're designed for companies with:
- Standard hierarchies
- Predictable workflows  
- Enterprise budgets
- Full-time IT staff

But what if you're:
- A nonprofit with volunteer coordinators and grant-based funding?
- An arts collective managing member applications and exhibition submissions?
- A small business with a unique service model that doesn't fit Salesforce?

**You get stuck adapting your work to match the software**, rather than the other way around.

## When Custom Software Makes Sense

Custom software isn't for everyone. But it's the right choice when:

1. **Your workflow is your competitive advantage**  
   If how you work is what makes you special, don't force it into generic templates.

2. **You're paying for features you'll never use**  
   Why pay $50/user/month when you only need 20% of the features?

3. **Integration is a nightmare**  
   Zapier subscriptions, broken webhooks, and data living in five different places.

4. **Your clients need access**  
   Client portals shouldn't require enterprise licenses for everyone.

## Real Example: Client Portals

I recently built a client portal for a consulting practice. The requirements were simple:
- Clients log in with magic links (no passwords)
- View project updates, files, and messages
- Approve deliverables  
- Track project journey visually

**Cost with off-the-shelf tools?** Combining a project management tool + client access + file storage + authentication = $200+/month for 3-5 clients.

**Cost of custom solution?** Built on Supabase and Next.js. Hosting: ~$10/month. No per-user fees. Fully tailored UX. Clients love it.

## The Real Benefits

### 1. **You Own Your Data**
No vendor lock-in. Export anything, anytime. Move hosting if you want.

### 2. **It Grows With You**  
Need a new feature? Add it. No waiting for the product team to prioritize your niche use case.

### 3. **Simpler for Users**  
Show only what they need. No bloated interfaces or irrelevant features.

### 4. **Integration on Your Terms**  
Connect exactly what you need. No middleware subscriptions or API rate limits.

## But What About Cost?

"Custom software must be expensive, right?"

Not necessarily. Here's the reality:

- **Small tools** (client portals, dashboards, internal tools): $3k-$8k one-time build
- **SaaS alternative**: $150-$500/month = $1,800-$6,000/year *forever*

After 1-2 years, custom software often costs less than ongoing SaaS subscriptions—and you own it.

## What to Consider

Custom software **isn't** right if:
- Your needs are truly generic (email, accounting, etc.)
- You change processes constantly and need vendor-driven updates
- You don't have a clear picture of what you need

Custom software **is** right if:
- Your workflow is stable and unique
- You're tired of paying for unused features
- You need something your clients/members can access easily
- Integration and data ownership matter

## Getting Started

If you're exploring custom software:

1. **Map your current workflow**—what actually happens, not what the software forces
2. **Identify pain points**—where do workarounds happen?
3. **Prioritize features**—what's mission-critical vs. nice-to-have?
4. **Talk to someone who gets it**—not a big agency, but someone who understands small-org constraints

Custom software isn't about building the next Facebook. It's about building exactly what you need, nothing more, nothing less.
